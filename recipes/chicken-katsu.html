Chicken Katsu

Description

Chicken katsu is a popular Japanese dish consisting of a boneless chicken cutlet (breast or thigh) pounded thin, breaded in airy panko breadcrumbs, and deep-fried until golden and crispy.

Often called a "Japanese schnitzel," it is typically served with a savory, thick tonkatsu sauce, shredded cabbage, and steamed rice.

Ingredients

  • 2 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves - pounded to ½-inch thickness
  • Seasoning (salt and peper) to taste
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • ½ large egg, beaten
  • ½ cup panko bread crumbs
  • ½ cup oil for frying, or as needed

What to serve with the chicken?

  • Tonkatsu sauce
  • Coconut rice
  • Sesame green beans
  • Sunomono

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Season chicken breasts on both sides with salt and pepper.
  3. Place flour, beaten egg, and panko crumbs into separate shallow dishes.
  4. Coat chicken breasts in flour, shaking off any excess; dip into egg, and then press into panko crumbs until well coated on both sides.
  5. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Place chicken in the hot oil, and fry until golden brown, 3 or 4 minutes per side. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate to drain.
  6. Serve and enjoy.
